Year 3 Applied Electricity - Electrical Energy Supply: Generating Station (Ghana)

Generating Station Content (Ghana)

1. Introduction to Generating Stations in Ghana

Ghana's electricity generation sector is diverse and evolving, with a mix of hydroelectric, thermal, and renewable energy sources. The country has been working towards increasing its power generation capacity to meet growing demand and support economic development.

2. Types of Generating Stations in Ghana

  • Hydroelectric Power Plants: Ghana's largest source of electricity, including the Akosombo Dam and Bui Dam.
  • Thermal Power Plants: Using natural gas, light crude oil, or diesel, such as the Tema Thermal Plant Complex.
  • Solar Power Plants: Emerging renewable energy source, with projects like the BXC Solar Power Station in the Central Region.
  • Biomass Power Plants: Potential future source, with planned projects using cocoa pod husks and other agricultural waste.

3. Major Power Plants in Ghana

  1. Akosombo Hydroelectric Power Station:
    • Capacity: 1,020 MW
    • Location: Eastern Region
    • Commissioned: 1965
    • Operator: Volta River Authority (VRA)
  2. Bui Hydroelectric Power Station:
    • Capacity: 400 MW
    • Location: Brong Ahafo Region
    • Commissioned: 2013
    • Operator: Bui Power Authority
  3. Takoradi Thermal Power Station:
    • Capacity: 330 MW
    • Location: Western Region
    • Fuel: Light Crude Oil / Natural Gas
    • Operator: Volta River Authority (VRA)
  4. Kpone Thermal Power Plant:
    • Capacity: 350 MW
    • Location: Greater Accra Region
    • Fuel: Natural Gas
    • Operator: Volta River Authority (VRA)
  5. Cenpower Kpone Independent Power Plant:
    • Capacity: 350 MW
    • Location: Greater Accra Region
    • Fuel: Multi-fuel (Natural Gas / Diesel)
    • Operator: Cenpower Generation Company Limited

4. Challenges in Ghana's Power Generation Sector

  • Overreliance on hydroelectric power: Vulnerable to rainfall patterns and climate change.
  • Fuel supply issues for thermal power plants: Inconsistent natural gas supply and high costs of light crude oil.
  • Aging infrastructure: Many plants require renovation and upgrades.
  • Transmission and distribution losses: High technical and commercial losses in the power system.
  • Financing: Difficulty in securing investments for new projects and maintenance.

5. Future of Power Generation in Ghana

Ghana is focusing on diversifying its energy mix and increasing capacity:

  • Renewable Energy: Aim to increase the share of renewable energy (excluding large hydro) to 10% by 2030.
  • Nuclear Power: Plans to introduce nuclear power to the energy mix in the long term.
  • Natural Gas Infrastructure: Developing infrastructure to utilize domestic natural gas resources.
  • Independent Power Producers (IPPs): Encouraging private sector participation to increase generation capacity.
  • Regional Power Integration: Participating in the West African Power Pool (WAPP) for cross-border electricity trade.

Work Solutions

Problem 1: Akosombo Dam Efficiency

The Akosombo Dam has a maximum water flow rate of 1,600 m³/s and a head of 69 meters. If the overall efficiency of the hydroelectric plant is 90%, calculate the maximum power output.

Solution:

  1. The power available in falling water is given by the formula:
    P = ρ × g × Q × H × η
    Where:
    ρ = density of water (1000 kg/m³)
    g = acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/s²)
    Q = flow rate (1,600 m³/s)
    H = head (69 m)
    η = efficiency (0.90)
  2. Calculate the power output:
    P = 1000 × 9.81 × 1600 × 69 × 0.90
    P = 973,555,200 W = 973.6 MW

Therefore, the maximum power output of the Akosombo Dam is approximately 973.6 MW.

Problem 2: Takoradi Thermal Power Station Fuel Consumption

The Takoradi Thermal Power Station has a capacity of 330 MW and operates at an efficiency of 35%. If the station runs at full capacity for 24 hours, calculate the amount of fuel oil consumed, given that the calorific value of the fuel oil is 42 MJ/kg.

Solution:

  1. Calculate the total energy output in 24 hours:
    Energy output = 330 MW × 24 hours = 7,920 MWh = 28,512,000 MJ
  2. Calculate the total energy input required:
    Energy input = Energy output / Efficiency
    Energy input = 28,512,000 MJ / 0.35 = 81,462,857 MJ
  3. Calculate the amount of fuel oil consumed:
    Fuel oil consumed = Energy input / Calorific value
    Fuel oil consumed = 81,462,857 MJ / 42 MJ/kg = 1,939,592 kg ≈ 1,940 tonnes

Therefore, the Takoradi Thermal Power Station would consume approximately 1,940 tonnes of fuel oil in 24 hours of full capacity operation.

Problem 3: Solar Power Plant Output

A solar power plant in Ghana has 10,000 photovoltaic panels, each rated at 300W. If the average daily sunshine duration is 6 hours and the overall system efficiency is 80%, calculate the daily energy output of the plant.

Solution:

  1. Calculate the total installed capacity:
    Total capacity = Number of panels × Panel rating
    Total capacity = 10,000 × 300W = 3,000,000W = 3 MW
  2. Calculate the daily energy output:
    Energy output = Total capacity × Sunshine hours × Efficiency
    Energy output = 3 MW × 6 hours × 0.80
    Energy output = 14.4 MWh

Therefore, the daily energy output of the solar power plant is 14.4 MWh.

Projects

1. Mini Hydroelectric Generator

Objective: Build a small-scale hydroelectric generator to understand the principles of hydropower.

Materials needed:

  • PVC pipe and fittings
  • Small DC motor
  • LED lights
  • Plastic spoons or small turbine blades
  • Water source (e.g., elevated bucket or tap)

Steps:

  1. Construct a water wheel using plastic spoons or small turbine blades.
  2. Attach the water wheel to the DC motor shaft.
  3. Create a housing for the water wheel using PVC pipes and fittings.
  4. Connect LED lights to the motor output.
  5. Test the generator with flowing water and measure the output.

2. Solar-Powered Water Pump

Objective: Design a solar-powered water pump system for small-scale irrigation, similar to those used in rural Ghana.

Materials needed:

  • Solar panel (5-10W)
  • Small water pump (12V DC)
  • Water storage container
  • PVC pipes and fittings
  • Charge controller (optional)

Steps:

  1. Connect the solar panel to the water pump (use a charge controller if available).
  2. Set up the water storage container and piping system.
  3. Install the pump in the water source.
  4. Test the system's performance under different sunlight conditions.
  5. Measure the water flow rate and calculate the system's efficiency.

3. Biomass Briquette Production

Objective: Create biomass briquettes using agricultural waste (e.g., cocoa pod husks) for small-scale power generation.

Materials needed:

  • Agricultural waste (e.g., sawdust, rice husks, or cocoa pod husks)
  • Binder (e.g., cassava starch)
  • Briquette press (can be handmade or purchased)
  • Drying racks

Steps:

  1. Collect and prepare the agricultural waste materials.
  2. Mix the waste with the binder to create a consistent mixture.
  3. Use the briquette press to form the briquettes.
  4. Dry the briquettes thoroughly.
  5. Test the briquettes for burning efficiency and compare with traditional fuels.
